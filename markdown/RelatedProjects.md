## US National Flood Interoperability Experiment (NFIE) 2014-2016

A team of researchers funded by NSF, the US National Weather Service and CUAHSI developed high-resolution, real-time streamflow forecasting capability across the continental US. Details on NFIE and the 2015 Summer Institute are posted by CUAHSI here: \<a href="<https://www.cuahsi.org/NFIE>" target="\_blank"\><https://www.cuahsi.org/NFIE>\</a\>. The 2016 Summer Institute info is here: \<a href="<https://www.cuahsi.org/summerinstitute>" target="\_blank"\><https://www.cuahsi.org/summerinstitute>\</a\>.

## GEOSS Architecture Implementation Pilot (AIP) - Water Societal Benefit Area, 2012-2015

From 2012 - 2015, outreach for use of <a href="WaterML" class="wikilink">WaterML</a> 2 and OGC web services has been conducted as the Water Societal Benefit Area (SBA) thread of the GEOSS Architecture Implementation Pilot (AIP). Announcements and results are posted on \<a href="<http://www.ogcnetwork.net/AIP>" target="\_blank"\>OGCNetwork.net\</a\>. ## OWSLib / geopython

OWSLib is a Python package for client programming with Open Geospatial Consortium (OGC) web service (hence OWS) interface standards, and their related content models.

<http://geopython.github.io/OWSLib>

## WMLViewer

WMLViewer is a simple set of <a href="JavaScript" class="wikilink">JavaScript</a> tools for viewing OGC <a href="WaterML" class="wikilink">WaterML</a> 2.0 datasets as maps and graphs.

<https://github.com/crwr/wmlviewer>

## RDA Global Water Information Interest Group (GWIIG)

The [GWIIG](https://www.rd-alliance.org/groups/global-water-information-interest-group.html-0) enables communication among RDA WGs and IGs focused on both technical eInfrastructure aspects (e.g. brokering; metadata and catalogs, dynamic data citation) and on related research domains (e.g. geosciences, agriculture, biodiversity) that are pertinent to water and water data.
