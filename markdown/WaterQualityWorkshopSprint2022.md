#### 29, 30 & 31 March 2022, Online

\<h3 id='Objectives'\>Objectives\</h3\>

The Opening Workshop was the first of a series of workshops on Water Quality Monitoring taking place during 2022-2023. It was intended to contribute to WMO’s Water Quality Long-Term ambition and fed into various activities from the key partners such as

1.  \<p\>Identifying potential synergies and mutual contributions between WMO-UNEP-WHO-UNESCO on in-situ water quality data observations, management and sharing.\</p\>
2.  \<p\>Defining a WMO-UNEP-WHO-UNESCO coordination mechanism on in-situ water quality data observations, management and sharing.\</p\>
3.  \<p\>Identifying the steps for the development of a joint WMO-UNEP-WHO-UNESCO work plan for regulatory and guidance material related to in-situ monitoring systems, from data collection to data sharing and use.\</p\>
4.  \<p\>Identifying the way of interconnecting WHOS with GEMStat and other existing in-situ water quality data platforms and systems and how to connect these to the World Water Quality Assessment under development.\</p\>
5.  \<p\>Identifying how to support the <a href="WaterML" class="wikilink">WaterML</a> development in the domain of Water Quality: updating OGC <a href="WaterML" class="wikilink">WaterML</a> -WQ Best Practice document with regards to evolutions in OGC APIs, and taxonomies/ontologies since 2014 and endorse an international standard on Water Quality data exchange.\</p\>
6.  \<p\>Defining the Roadmap for an international OGC Interoperability Experiment on Water Quality data with pilot use cases (e.g., Lake Victoria, Lake Chad).\</p\>
